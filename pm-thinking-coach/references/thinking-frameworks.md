# Thinking Frameworks

## Core PM Equation

```text
Product performance + Market demand = Product value
Market demand = User need + Competitor performance
```

Use it as a causal chain, not a slogan:

1. What user behavior or business result changed?
2. Which user need or market constraint explains the change?
3. Why did this product's specific performance beat or fail against alternatives?
4. What value was created for user, platform, and company?

## Product Performance

Ask:

- What scenario is this feature solving?
- Why is this scenario worth solving now?
- What user path changed before and after the product decision?
- Which metric proves contribution to team or company OKR?
- What did the PM contribute beyond execution: problem definition, strategy, prioritization, cross-functional alignment, experiment design, metric movement?
- Which metric can go up while the product experience quietly gets worse?

## Market Demand

Break demand into user need and competitor performance:

- Target users: core user, expanding user, payer, influencer, operator, ecosystem partner.
- Need depth: pain intensity, frequency, willingness to switch/pay, urgency, emotional stakes.
- Competitors/substitutes: direct competitors, workarounds, offline/manual alternatives, `do nothing`.
- Positioning: why this product should win in this segment now.
- Timing: platform shift, regulation, model capability, cost curve, distribution channel, social behavior.
- Demand proof: search behavior, retention, repeat usage, paid conversion, user workaround, competitive copying.

## Product Value

Evaluate value at three levels:

- User: real pain solved, efficiency, experience, cost reduction, confidence, status, creativity.
- Platform: retention, engagement, content supply, network effects, data quality, ecosystem lock-in.
- Business: revenue, conversion, margin, sales motion, commercial loop, durable moat.

## Decision and Cognition Lenses

Use these selectively, not as decoration:

- Kahneman/Tversky: base rates, loss aversion, availability bias, anchoring, overconfidence. Ask whether the team is overweighting vivid anecdotes or recent wins.
- Scarcity: attention bandwidth, tunneling, slack, urgency pressure. Ask what scarce resource the user or company is protecting.
- Cialdini: social proof, authority, commitment, reciprocity, scarcity, liking, unity. Ask whether adoption is driven by real utility or persuasion cues.
- Taleb/asymmetric risk: upside/downside distribution, fragility, hidden exposure, incentives. Ask who bears the downside if the product bet fails.
- Fogg Behavior Model: motivation, ability, prompt. If behavior does not happen, identify the missing element.
- Critical thinking: claim, evidence, assumption, counterexample, alternative explanation, what would change my mind.

## Product Judgment Moves

Use questions that force product judgment:

- What would have to be true for this strategy to work?
- Which user segment benefits, and who pays the cost?
- Which competitor is the product choosing not to fight?
- What is the strongest competitor response?
- If the product has only one scarce resource, what should it protect?
- What is the non-obvious reason users might resist this feature?
- What evidence would make you reverse your view?
- What can be tested cheaply before the team commits deeply?

## Personal Work Reflection

When coaching the user's own work, always pull from execution back to contribution:

- `Direction`: Why was this problem worth solving at this stage?
- `Discovery`: How was the scenario found? What evidence made it real?
- `Choice`: What did you choose not to do, and why?
- `Contribution`: What part of the result depended on your judgment?
- `Metric`: Which indicator connects to team/company OKR?
- `Behavior`: What did users do differently after the change?
- `Learning`: What changed in your product belief after the project?

## Writing Template

Use this for the user's final thinking output:

```markdown
我观察到：[product/user behavior/fact]
我判断核心问题是：[real user need or strategic constraint]
关键证据是：[source, metric, user path, competitor signal]
我的产品假设是：[if we do X, user/company outcome Y changes because Z]
风险在于：[bias, competitor, adoption, cost, trust, incentive]
下一步验证：[experiment, metric, research, launch slice]
```
